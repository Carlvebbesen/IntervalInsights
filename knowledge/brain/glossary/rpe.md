# RPE — Rate of Perceived Exertion

> A subjective 1–10 (or 6–20 Borg) scale rating how hard an effort *feels*.

## Key facts

- Cheap, equipment-free way to gauge intensity; correlates with HR and load.
- Maps roughly onto `[[training-zones]]`: easy ≈ 3–4, threshold ≈ 7–8, VO₂max ≈ 9–10.
- Useful when HR is unreliable (heat, fatigue, cardiac drift) or unavailable.

## Caveat: the least-reliable control

- In `[[control-intensity-by-measurement]]`, perceived effort ranks **below** lactate, heart
  rate, and the **talk test** (at threshold you should manage short sentences, not a comfortable
  conversation). The cruder the tool, the bigger the safety margin you must leave — and the more
  valuable a `[[lactate-testing|lactate meter]]` becomes for policing a narrow threshold band.
