# Marius Bakken

> Norwegian 5000 m runner (PB **13:06**, Rome 2004) and self-experimenter whose
> lactate-controlled double-threshold system became known as the **[[norwegian-method]]**.

## Key facts

- **5000 m PB 13:06** (fastest European time of 2004); **9th at the 2001 World Championships.**
- Ran **5,500+ in-field lactate tests**; identified the **~2.3–3.0 mmol/L** sub-threshold sweet
  spot (vs the textbook 4.0). Pioneered [[double-threshold]] training and the [[lactate-testing]]
  "traffic-light" / lactate-curve method.
- **Influences he synthesized:** Joe Newton (25 × 400 m, 160 km/wk), **Peter Coe** (double
  threshold, hills), **Bob Kennedy** (1000 + 300 race-pace test), Per Halle (1972 training
  diary), **Frank Evertsen** (lactate testing + Kenyan study), Leif Olav Alnes (clustering,
  sprint), Alex Stanton (flat weekly structure).
- His system reached the **Ingebrigtsen** brothers via coach **Eric Toogood** (and Bjørnar
  Kristensen) around 2011; also used by triathletes Blummenfelt & Iden.
- Now a **medical doctor**; 2009 master's thesis on **[[muscle-tone]]** and cross-friction
  massage. Author of *The Norwegian Method Applied*.
