# Sodium Bicarbonate

> An ingestible **buffer** taken before high-intensity work to neutralise hydrogen ions (H⁺) and
> delay the acidosis that limits hard, anaerobic efforts. Maurten sells it as the "Bicarb System."

## Key facts

- Buffers the **metabolic acidosis** produced by anaerobic work — relevant to **lactate-tolerance,
  VO₂ max, and hard threshold** reps, where H⁺ accumulation (associated with rising blood lactate,
  see `[[lactate-threshold]]`) is the limiter. It is an **ergogenic aid**, not an energy source —
  distinct from `[[carbohydrate-fueling]]`.
- **Timing:** taken **~1–4 h before** the session (`[[src-maurten-fuel-guides]]`); the classic
  research dose is **~0.2–0.3 g/kg body mass** (sports-nutrition consensus, *not* stated in the source).
- **GI distress** is the main downside of plain bicarbonate; encapsulated/hydrogel delivery (the
  Maurten system) aims to reduce it.
- Where it fits in a plan: the **pre-session** column for high-intensity rows in `[[fueling-by-session-type]]`.
- One of the **few evidence-backed ergogenic aids** — see `[[dietary-supplements]]` for the
  food-first framing and the doping/contamination caveat.
