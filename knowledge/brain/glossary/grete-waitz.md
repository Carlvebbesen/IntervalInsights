# Grete Waitz

> Norwegian distance legend (**9× NYC Marathon**, 1983 marathon World Champion) who built
> endurance on **steady sub-threshold running plus VO₂max work** — a forerunner of the
> [[norwegian-method]].

## Key facts

- **PBs:** 1500 m 4:00.55, 3000 m 8:31.75; **four marathon world records**; two 3000 m WRs.
- **Volume ~160 km/wk** (200+ in winter, 140–150 in summer; peak week 180), through her best
  track years (1978–79).
- **Built endurance via sub-AT running, not slow jogging:** estimated AT pace ~**3:25/km**;
  "easy" runs **3:30–4:00/km** and "extremely steady" — she and Ingrid Kristiansen *"almost
  never saw the 4-something/km"* on easy days. Often trained with men.
- **Signature sessions:** 4 × 1000 m (1 min rest), **25 × 300 m (15 s rest)** — see
  [[waitz-25x300m]] — and 1000-600-300 track ladders.
- Coached in her marathon era by **Johan Kaggestad**. Per [[marius-bakken]]: she and Kristiansen
  *"built endurance through sub-AT running — much like what we see in today's Kenyans and
  Moroccans, combined with fast VO₂max sessions."*
