# During-Training Nutrition

> What to take *in* during a session to hold blood glucose, spare glycogen, and delay fatigue. The
> "during" stage of fuelling timing — the applied home of `[[carbohydrate-fueling]]` + `[[hydration]]`.

## How it works — per `[[src-olt-during-training]]`

- **< 90 min:** fluid if **> 30 min**; carbs usually optional (unless high-intensity intervals or
  already glycogen-depleted).
- **≥ 90 min:** **30–60 g carbohydrate/hour** + fluid; **start within the first 30 min**, then top
  up every **15–20 min**.
- **Fluid:** **0.5–1 L/h** in even sips; drink **before** thirst; adjust for heat/intensity
  (`[[hydration]]`).
- **Big losses need salt + carbs**, not water alone (sports-drink model).
- **Ultra-endurance (< 70% max HR):** same carb targets, but **solid food is tolerated** (müsli
  bars, bread + jam, warm soup); liquid meals suit cyclists/triathletes.

## When to use it

- Any session beyond ~60–90 min, and all long competition — bracketed by `[[pre-training-nutrition]]`
  and `[[post-training-nutrition]]`. Per-session targets: `[[fueling-by-session-type]]`. To exceed
  ~60 g/h, combine glucose + fructose (`[[multiple-transportable-carbohydrates]]`).

## Evidence & caveats

- **Train the gut** and **trial what suits you** — **never** try new food/drink in an important
  competition.
- High-GI carbs are preferred for sustained efforts.
