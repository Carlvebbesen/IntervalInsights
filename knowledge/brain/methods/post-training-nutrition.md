# Post-Training Nutrition

> Recovery fuelling: replace **carbohydrate, protein, and fluid** to restock glycogen and repair
> muscle. The "after" stage of timing — the practical edge of `[[recovery-and-adaptation]]`.

## How it works — per `[[src-olt-post-training]]`

- **Carbohydrate:** **1–1.5 g/kg within 2 h** when the next session is < 8 h away or you train
  multiple times/day; at least **30–50 g** after a single daily session. Glycogen refills at
  **~5%/h** → full restock takes **~20–24 h**.
- **Protein:** **10–20 g** high-quality protein to drive muscle protein synthesis (`[[protein-for-athletes]]`).
- **Fluid:** replace **~150% of body mass lost** (1 kg → 1.5 L); use **sodium-containing** drinks —
  low-sodium fluid just increases urine output (`[[hydration]]`).
- **Timing:** small intake within **30 min**, then spread every **15–30 min over 2 h** for best
  absorption.

## When to use it

- After every meaningful session — **most urgent before a second session** (e.g. the AM→PM gap of a
  `[[double-threshold]]` day) or the next day's quality. Race-week version: `[[glycogen-loading]]`;
  competition version: `[[competition-day-nutrition]]`. Then return to normal `[[meal-pattern]]`.

## Evidence & caveats

- "Carbohydrate is the most important nutrient for recovery" (acute *and* long-term) per the source.
- Prefer **moderate-to-high GI** carbs when turnaround is short; relax that for a single daily session.
