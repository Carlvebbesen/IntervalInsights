# Time-Zone Acclimatization

> Managing **jet lag** so it doesn't blunt competition. Crossing time zones can "significantly
> reduce both physical and cognitive performance" (`[[src-olt-time-zone]]`) — plan the adjustment
> into the trip, don't wing it.

## How it works

- **Use the Olympiatoppen time-zone app** (`olt-app.no/tidssone/`): it generates an individualised
  light-exposure and sleep schedule for your specific route and competition time. This is the
  source's primary recommendation.
- **General chronobiology (consensus, _not_ from the OLT source):**
  - Rough adjustment is **~1 day per time zone crossed**; **eastward** travel is usually harder than
    westward.
  - **Light is the master clock cue** — seek/avoid daylight at the right times to shift the rhythm.
  - Anchor the new schedule fast: meals, training, and especially **`[[sleep]]`** timing on local time.

## When to use it

- Before any competition across **≥2–3 time zones** — build the adjustment window into
  `[[periodization]]` (arrive early enough to acclimatize) and combine with `[[travel-nutrition]]`
  and `[[competition-day-nutrition]]` on the ground.
