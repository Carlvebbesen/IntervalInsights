# Competition-Day Nutrition

> Fuelling on race day — including the hard case of **multiple heats/rounds** across a day or
> weekend: refuel between rounds without GI distress. Applies the timing stages to competition.

## How it works — per `[[src-olt-competition-food]]`

- **Keep the meal rhythm:** 4 meals/day, **≤3–4 h** apart, even on competition day.
- **Pre-competition:** carbohydrate-rich (bread, grains, pasta, potatoes) + lean protein + veg
  (see `[[pre-training-nutrition]]`; race-week loading via `[[glycogen-loading]]`).
- **During:** ≤1 h → water; **>1 h at moderate–high intensity** → diluted juice / sports drink
  (carb + electrolytes) — `[[during-training-nutrition]]`, `[[hydration]]`.
- **Between heats/rounds:** portable, easily digestible carbs — **banana, nuts + dried fruit,
  homemade energy bars** — restore glycogen without gut upset.
- **Recovery between/after:** chocolate milk, smoothies, yogurt + grains, bread + protein topping
  (`[[post-training-nutrition]]`).

## When to use it

- Any competition day; essential for **tournament / multi-heat** formats. When the event is abroad,
  pair with `[[travel-nutrition]]` and `[[time-zone-acclimatization]]`.

## Evidence & caveats

- **No unfamiliar foods on competition day** — only rehearsed options.
- **Nerves slow digestion** — favour familiar, easily digested foods and plan extra hydration
  (large fluid need, especially indoors/heat).
