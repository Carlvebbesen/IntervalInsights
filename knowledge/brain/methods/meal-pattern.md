# Meal Pattern & Food Choices

> The everyday eating structure under all training: **regular meals, stable blood glucose, food
> variety**. The daily base layer of `[[performance-nutrition]]` that session-timing sits on top of.

## How it works — per `[[src-olt-meal-pattern]]`

- **Frequency:** **4 main meals/day + snacks**; **no more than 3–4 h** between energy intakes.
- **Plate model (dinner):** combine **protein-rich + carbohydrate-rich + vegetables** in balanced
  proportions.
- **Variety:** eat across all **seven food groups** daily (bread/grains; potato/rice/pasta;
  vegetables; fruit/berries; fish/poultry/meat/egg; dairy; fats) — this is also how you hit
  `[[antioxidants]]` (≥5 portions produce) and `[[protein-for-athletes]]` distribution.
- **Recommended daily supplements:** **fish oil / omega-3** + **vitamin D**.

## When to use it

- Always — it's the foundation. Stable meal rhythm protects training intensity and underpins
  adequate `[[energy-availability]]`; post-session it flows from `[[post-training-nutrition]]` back
  to normal meals. It's also the frame for managing `[[lactose-intolerance]]`, `[[food-allergy]]`,
  and `[[gluten-and-celiac]]` without nutrient gaps.

## Evidence & caveats

- Large blood-sugar swings cause fatigue, poor concentration, more muscle breakdown, headache, and
  mood swings; poor meal timing compromises the next session.
