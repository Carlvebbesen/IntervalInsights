# Heat Acclimatization

> Deliberately training in heat for ~2 weeks before a hot race so the body adapts — the
> environmental-prep sibling of `[[altitude-training]]` and `[[time-zone-acclimatization]]`.

## How it works

- **Dose:** ~**10–14 days** of **60–90 min/day** exercising in the heat (active heat acclimation),
  optionally extended with post-session passive heat (hot bath / sauna).
- **Adaptations:** expanded **plasma volume**, earlier and greater **sweating**, lower sweat
  sodium, and lower heart rate and core temperature at a given effort — more headroom before heat
  strain (`[[hydration]]`).
- **Decay:** the adaptation fades over **~1–3 weeks** after heat exposure stops, so time the block
  to finish at the competition.

## When to use it

- Before competition in hot/humid conditions — the kind of climate acclimatization the Norwegian
  Olympic program ran for Tokyo (`[[src-olt-endurance-development-pod]]`). Build it into the
  `[[periodization]]` run-in, the way travel prep uses `[[time-zone-acclimatization]]`.

## Evidence & caveats

- Protocol specifics (days, plasma-volume/sweat changes, decay) are **heat-physiology consensus**
  (Périard, Racinais et al.) — the OLT source only names climate acclimatization as part of the
  model, with no numbers.
- Heat training **adds physiological load** — fit it carefully alongside quality work, and lean
  hard on `[[hydration]]` and sodium (a hot block is exactly when **"salty sweaters"** need extra salt).
