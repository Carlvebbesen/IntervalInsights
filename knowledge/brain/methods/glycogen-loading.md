# Glycogen Loading

> Maximise muscle glycogen before a long competition by **tapering training while raising carbohydrate
> intake** — "supercompensation." Worth ~**2–3% more work capacity** on events **≥90 min**.

## How it works — per `[[src-olt-glycogen-loading]]`

1. **Build the daily base** (periodise carbs to load):
   | Daily training | Carbohydrate |
   |---|---|
   | < 60–90 min | **5–7 g/kg/day** |
   | 90–120 min | **7–10 g/kg/day** |
   | extreme (6–8 h) | **10–12 g/kg/day** |
2. **Load into the race:** **[[tapering|taper]] training over 1–4 days** while eating **~10 g/kg/day** carbohydrate.
3. **Refuel fast** day-to-day: **1–1.5 g carb/kg within 30 min** post-session (high-GI) — glycogen
   restores at ~5%/h, so full restock takes ~20–24 h (see `[[post-training-nutrition]]`).

## When to use it

- Endurance events **≥90 min** (full stores last only ~1–2 h, beyond which you also need in-race
  carbs — `[[carbohydrate-fueling]]`). Slot the load into the `[[periodization]]` taper before a
  key race, alongside `[[competition-day-nutrition]]`.

## Evidence & caveats

- **The depletion phase is obsolete** — modern loading skips it; just taper + eat carbs.
- **Loading adds body mass** (glycogen binds water) — expected, not fat; usually a net win in long
  events but worth knowing for weight-sensitive sports.
- Applies the `[[fuel-for-the-work-required]]` logic at the scale of a race build.
