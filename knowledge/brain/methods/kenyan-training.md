# Kenyan Training (LT-Centered Base)

> Train the bulk of running **right below [[lactate-threshold]]** — "comfortably hard,"
> controlled, several times a week. The disciplined LT base is the "red line" running through
> East-African (and 1980s British / Moroccan) distance dominance.

This is the model [[marius-bakken]] reverse-engineered (with physiologist Frank Evertsen and
Bengt Saltin's data) and adapted into the [[norwegian-method]]: Kenyans hit the right intensity
*by feel*; Westerners must use a meter to copy it.

## How it works

- **Base = work just below LT** (~0.3–0.5 mmol/L below personal LT); touch LT only on the last
  rep, if at all. **Too much work *at* LT causes overtraining.**
- **"Comfortably hard."** Finish a session able to do 2–3 more reps. Never run to exhaustion —
  the "no pain, no gain" maxim does **not** apply to LT work. Discipline over heroics.
- **LT sessions several times per week**, 36–60 min of work, e.g. **4–5 × 10 min** or
  **7 × 6 min** ([[10min-threshold-reps]]); start easy, accelerate into LT.
- **Long run = "footing,"** 45–60 min finishing at LT — deceptively easy early, then high
  intensity.
- **Track sessions:** regular hard work at **4–8 mmol/L**; racing season only **>10**. You want
  a **steady, gradual lactate rise across reps** (a sign of control and absorption), not a
  spike — *not* "dying" on the track. E.g. a 1200/800 ladder climbing 5.8 → 9.3 mmol/L; or the
  descending [[descending-1600-400-track]].
- **Measure, don't guess** ([[control-intensity-by-measurement]]): lactate at a given pace is
  stable day-to-day; "feeling comfortable/hard is simply not good enough — you need to KNOW."

## Lactate zones (long-distance Kenyan model)

| Zone | Lactate | Use |
|------|---------|-----|
| Z1 | <1.5 mmol/L | easy |
| Z2 | 1.5–2.0 | steady |
| **Z3 (LT)** | **2.0–3.0** | the base |
| Z4 | 3.0–8.0 | track sessions |
| Z5 | >8.0 | tempo / lactic, race season |

Typical LT ≈ **3.0 mmol/L** (range 3–4) for long-distance, ≈ **4.5** for middle-distance. (A
3:46 1500 m runner tested at LT ≈ 2.4 mmol/L at 16.8 km/h.) See [[training-zones]].

## Moroccan variant (El Guerrouj / Salah Hissou, coach Abdelkader Kada)

Built on Peter Coe's system. **AE (aerobic-endurance) sessions at 2:50–3:10/km for 30–45 min**
*are* LT work for elites. Race-pace = fartlek **6/5/4/3/2/1 min** or the descending
**1600/1200/800/600/400** track ladder. Power = 10 × 300 m hills or multi-jump circuits.

## When to use it

- As the year-round aerobic engine for distance runners. Note: Kenyans abroad in racing season
  (2–3 months) show *little* LT work — the LT base is built at home, not in the racing block.

## Caveats

- The full Kenyan package also leans on **moderate [[altitude-training]] (2000–2600 m)**,
  innate [[running-economy]], soft terrain, and year-round racing from childhood — see
  [[src-young-kenyan-training]] — much of which Western programs can't replicate.
