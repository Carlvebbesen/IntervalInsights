# Pre-Training Nutrition

> What and **when** to eat *before* a session: top up liver/muscle glycogen and steady blood glucose
> so the session's quality is there from the first rep. The "before" stage of fuelling timing.

## How it works — per `[[src-olt-pre-training]]`

- **Timing:**
  - Main meal **2–3 h before**.
  - Light meal **~1 h before**.
  - Early-morning session → small breakfast **30 min–1 h before**.
- **Composition:** **carbohydrate-rich, moderate-to-low GI**, moderate protein, **low fibre & fat**.
- **Why it matters:** an overnight fast empties liver glycogen → risk of low blood sugar; a
  moderate/low-GI carb feed keeps glucose normal and tops muscle glycogen for high-intensity work
  (see `[[carbohydrate-fueling]]`).

## When to use it

- Before any quality session and every competition — chain it into
  `[[during-training-nutrition]]` and `[[post-training-nutrition]]`. For per-session specifics
  (and pre-session `[[bicarbonate]]` for hard work) see `[[fueling-by-session-type]]`.

## Evidence & caveats

- **Avoid high-fat / high-fibre / high-protein meals** right before exercise (slow digestion, GI
  distress). Large volumes hurt running more than swimming/cycling.
- **Never trial new pre-race foods** — rehearse the routine in training.
- **Reactive hypoglycemia** after high-GI food → choose low-GI (yogurt, wholegrain bread, pasta,
  fruit) and do a brisk warm-up.
