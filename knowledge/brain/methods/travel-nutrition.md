# Travel Nutrition

> Food-safety and fuelling plan for **training camps and competition abroad** — keep the gut healthy
> and the home meal rhythm intact. Main risks: **traveler's diarrhea, dehydration, contaminated food.**

## How it works — per `[[src-olt-travel-abroad]]`

- **Prep the gut:** **1 dl probiotic yogurt/day** (e.g. Biola) *starting before departure*
  (`[[probiotics]]`).
- **In flight:** bring extra water; **avoid caffeine** (diuretic) — supports `[[hydration]]`.
- **Water hygiene** (if local water is doubtful): bottled water only — also for teeth and rinsing
  bottles; **no ice** from uncertain water.
- **Pack safe staples:** sports bars, cereal, canned/dried fruit, nuts, jam/honey/cheese, canned
  pasta, sports/rehydration drinks, juice, crackers.
- **Avoid:** raw meat, raw fish, shellfish, reheated food; poor-quality swimming water.
- **Plan ahead:** map supermarkets, hotel cooking facilities, and meal times to hold the home rhythm.

## When to use it

- Any trip abroad for training or competition — pair with `[[time-zone-acclimatization]]` for the
  circadian side and `[[competition-day-nutrition]]` on race day. Travelling with a
  `[[food-allergy]]` → pre-arrange safe food and carry your own.

## Evidence & caveats

- **Never share cups/bottles; isolate sick athletes** immediately.
- **If ill (nausea/diarrhea):** replace fluid + electrolytes; bland foods (dry bread, crackers);
  temporarily avoid fatty foods and dairy.
