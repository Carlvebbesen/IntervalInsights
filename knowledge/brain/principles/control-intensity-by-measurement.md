# Control Intensity by Measurement, Not Feel

> Set and police training intensity by what you can **measure** (lactate first, then HR, talk
> test, RPE) — not by feel — because feel drifts.

## Rationale

- The threshold model lives or dies on staying inside a **narrow sub-threshold band**; small
  intensity errors compound into overtraining.
- **Lactate at a given pace is stable** day-to-day; HR varies with sleep/fuel/heat; perceived
  effort is least reliable of all (see [[lactate-testing]]).
- Kenyan runners hit the right intensity by feel *naturally*; everyone else needs a meter to
  copy it — *"you need to KNOW."*

## How to apply

- **Order of reliability:** lactate > heart rate > **talk test** (short sentences, not a
  comfortable conversation) > [[rpe]].
- **The cruder the tool, the bigger the safety margin** you must leave.
- **When signals conflict, go easier** — *"being conservative when signals conflict is not
  caution; it is how the system works."*
- Use the 🟢🟡🔴 traffic-light and flat-vs-steep lactate-curve checks from [[lactate-testing]].
- **Two scales, both measured:** this principle governs the *intensity of a session*; the
  `[[performance-management-chart]]` (TSS/CTL/ATL/TSB) governs *cumulative load across weeks*. Use
  them together — but treat the load metrics as coarse "models, not measurements," and let lactate
  override them when they disagree.

## Violations to flag

> [!warning] Running a key threshold session on "comfortably hard" with no objective check;
> easy days creeping up in pace because they "felt fine"; pushing on when HR/lactate say stop.
