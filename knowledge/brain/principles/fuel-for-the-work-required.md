# Fuel for the Work Required

> Match carbohydrate intake to the session's metabolic demand — minimal for short/easy work,
> substantial for long or quality sessions — rather than fueling (or starving) every session the same.

## Rationale

- Glycogen cost scales with **duration × intensity**. A 40-min easy jog barely dents stores; a 28 km
  `[[progressive-long-run]]` or a hard threshold day does. Fueling should track that, not be uniform.
- Fueling **key sessions** protects the intended stimulus and pace (`[[carbohydrate-fueling]]`); a
  session run empty becomes a lower-quality, higher-fatigue session.
- The flip side: deliberately doing *some* low, easy aerobic work **without** extra carbohydrate can
  enhance fat-oxidation adaptations — but this is reserved for easy/base work, never for the
  sessions whose quality you care about.
- This is the *per-session* guardrail; its chronic, whole-diet companion is `[[energy-availability]]`
  (eat enough across the week), and its race-week extreme is `[[glycogen-loading]]`. Restrictive
  `[[alternative-diets]]` break all three.

## How to apply

| Session type | Fuel during |
|---|---|
| Easy / recovery, short (< ~60–75 min) | water; carbs **not** needed |
| Long run (> ~90 min) | **30–60 g/h** (≈ 1 gel/h) |
| Threshold / quality intervals | carbs **before**; sips between reps to hold pace |
| `[[double-threshold]]` day | refuel **hard between AM and PM** in race season |
| Racing (HM / marathon / long tri) | **60–90 g/h** |

Full per-session detail: `[[fueling-by-session-type]]`.

## Violations to flag

- `> [!warning]` Underfueling a **long run or key threshold day** → junk session, blunted adaptation,
  excess fatigue. Fix: fuel to protect the work.
- `> [!warning]` Loading carbs on **easy recovery jogs** → unnecessary, and forgoes the low-fuel
  aerobic adaptation those sessions can offer.
- `> [!warning]` Entering a **`[[double-threshold]]` PM session glycogen-empty *in race season*** →
  the evening quality suffers. (Note the nuance below — empty PM running is *intentional* in buildup.)

> [!note] Reconciled, not contradicted
> The Norwegian buildup tactic of running the **PM** double-threshold session in partial glycogen
> depletion (see `[[double-threshold]]`) is a *deliberate "train-low" choice for adaptation*, and it
> still respects this principle: in **race season** the same athletes refuel aggressively to protect
> quality. Train-low in buildup; fuel-for-performance when it counts.
