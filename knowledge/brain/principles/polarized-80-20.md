# The 80/20 Principle

> Roughly **80% easy / ~20% hard** — and in this brain the hard work **defaults to controlled
> threshold** (the pyramidal `[[norwegian-method]]`), *not* VO₂max-centric polarized.

## Rationale

- The ~80% easy share is shared by virtually all elite distance models — Seiler's polarized
  athletes (`[[src-seiler-2010]]`), the Norwegian/Kenyan threshold school, Grete Waitz. Lots of
  cheap easy volume builds the `[[aerobic-base]]`; keep easy genuinely easy so hard is hard.
- The disagreement is only about **where the hard ~20% goes**: toward VO₂max (polarized) or at
  controlled sub-threshold (threshold / pyramidal). This brain takes the **threshold side**.

## House default: threshold-first

> [!note] This brain's canonical intensity distribution
> Spend the hard ~20% **mostly on controlled, lactate-capped sub-threshold work (2–3 mmol/L)**,
> clustered into `[[double-threshold]]` days — sustainable at high frequency because it is
> *sub*-threshold (`[[muscular-load-limits-training]]`). This is the `[[norwegian-method]]`.
> Strict polarized (push the hard part to VO₂max, minimize all moderate work) is a valid,
> evidence-backed **alternative** (`[[polarized-training]]`) but is **not** the default here.
> VO₂max / race-pace work is a small late **"X element" / sharpening** tool, not the base staple.

## How to apply

- Keep **~80% genuinely easy** (Z1–Z2 of `[[training-zones]]`: <1.0 mmol/L, <70% HRmax).
- Spend the hard ~20% **at controlled threshold (2–3 mmol/L)**, as intervals, clustered, with a
  full easy day between hard days.
- Avoid the **uncontrolled grey zone** between easy and threshold — and don't let "easy" days
  drift up into it.
- Add a little VO₂max / race-pace work **late** (sharpening), not as the staple of the base.

## Violations to flag

> [!warning] (a) "Easy" days drifting into moderate/Z3 effort (the real grey-zone trap, and a
> conflict with `[[recovery-and-adaptation]]`). (b) Making **VO₂max intervals the base staple**
> instead of controlled threshold. (c) Running "threshold" work hard (well above ~3 mmol/L)
> every session — that's overtraining, not the model. (d) Less than ~80% easy by volume.
