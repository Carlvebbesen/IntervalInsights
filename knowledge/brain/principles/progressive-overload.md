# Progressive Overload

> To keep adapting, training stress must increase **gradually and systematically** over time.

## Rationale

- The body adapts to the load it's given; a constant load yields a plateau.
- Adaptation requires a stimulus slightly beyond current capacity, then recovery
  (`[[recovery-and-adaptation]]`) to supercompensate.

## How to apply

- Progress **one variable at a time**: volume, intensity, or density — not all at once.
- Common guidance: increase weekly load by a modest amount (~5–10%), then consolidate. A slowly
  rising `[[chronic-training-load]]` (CTL) is the quantitative signature of doing this right.
- Sequence progression across a plan via `[[periodization]]`.

## Violations to flag

> [!warning] Large week-over-week jumps in volume *and* intensity together, or no
> progression at all over many weeks, both break this principle.
