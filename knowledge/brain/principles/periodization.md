# Periodization

> Structure training into **phases** so that fitness peaks at the right time and stress is managed.

## Rationale

- You can't hold peak fitness indefinitely; training must build, sharpen, then taper.
- Phasing lets `[[progressive-overload]]` accumulate while controlling fatigue. On a
  `[[performance-management-chart]]` this is the build → peak arc: load drives fitness
  (`[[chronic-training-load|CTL]]`) up at the cost of negative form (`[[training-stress-balance|TSB]]`),
  which the `[[tapering|taper]]` then converts back into freshness for race day.
- **Systematicity and high volume are the cornerstone** of long-term endurance development:
  structured consistency is decisive and *"we don't get good at anything without training a lot"*
  (`[[src-olt-endurance-development-pod]]`).
- The peak/taper phase has a nutrition arm too — race-week `[[glycogen-loading]]` — and, for events
  abroad, build `[[time-zone-acclimatization]]` into the run-in.

## How to apply

- Classic sequence: **Base** (aerobic volume) → **Build** (intensity/threshold) →
  **Peak / [[tapering|taper]]** (race-specific sharpening, reduced volume) → **Recovery**.
- For hot or distant races, fold `[[heat-acclimatization]]` / `[[time-zone-acclimatization]]` into the run-in.
- Use recovery/down weeks (e.g. every 3–4 weeks) to absorb load.
- Make later phases increasingly `[[specificity|specific]]` to the goal event.

## Variations within the phase model

- **Flat weekly structure:** the modern `[[norwegian-method]]` keeps each winter week nearly
  identical and lets adaptation accumulate over the whole cycle, using `[[altitude-training]]`
  (not week-to-week hard/easy swings) as the main variation — inspired by Paula Radcliffe's coach.
- **Altitude peaking:** plan key races into the individual post-altitude window (day 1–3 or
  11–18 after descent). See `[[altitude-training]]`.

## Violations to flag

> [!warning] No phase structure (same week repeated year-round *with no peaking element*) or no
> taper before a key event indicates missing periodization.
