# Fractional Utilization

> The fraction of [[vo2max]] you can sustain at threshold (LT2) — often a **stronger predictor
> of distance performance than VO₂max itself.**

## Why it matters

- [[vo2max]] plateaus after years of training; raising the **% you can hold at LT2** (together
  with [[running-economy]]) is the more durable path to faster racing.
- This is the core rationale for the threshold-heavy [[double-threshold]] work in the
  [[norwegian-method]].

## Key facts

- *"Two athletes with identical VO₂max can race very differently if one can hold a faster
  velocity at LT2."*
- Pushing VO₂max higher requires work at **≥95 % VO₂max** — costly and hard to sustain.
  Improving fractional utilization is **more sustainable** for already-trained runners.
- Determined by the same adaptations threshold work targets: a high [[lactate-threshold]] as a
  fraction of aerobic ceiling.
