# Gluten & Celiac Disease

> In **celiac disease**, gluten triggers an autoimmune attack on the gut; a strict gluten-free diet
> is the treatment. There is **no evidence** a gluten-free diet helps non-celiac athletes.

## Why it matters

- Untreated celiac damages nutrient absorption and training capacity; a gluten-free athlete must
  still hit **carbohydrate** targets (`[[carbohydrate-fueling]]`) using non-wheat sources.
- For athletes *without* celiac, cutting gluten just narrows food choice for no benefit.

## Key facts — per `[[src-olt-gluten-celiac]]`

- **Untreated celiac** can cause joint/muscle problems, **bone weakening**, skin disease, and
  cancer; documented deficiencies in **B12, folate, vitamin D**.
- Gut inflammation can cause a **temporary secondary `[[lactose-intolerance]]`** (reduced lactase).
- **Carbs without wheat:** rice, potato, maize, certified gluten-free oats/grains and products keep
  glycogen topped up — plan via `[[meal-pattern]]`.
- Norwegian athletes can use **Norsk Cøliakiforening** for product and travel guidance.

> [!note] Diagnosis matters
> Get tested *before* removing gluten (a gluten-free diet can mask the diagnosis). Celiac is
> distinct from a wheat `[[food-allergy]]`.
