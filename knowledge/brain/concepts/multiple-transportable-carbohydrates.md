# Multiple Transportable Carbohydrates

> Combining **glucose + fructose** uses two *separate* intestinal transporters, letting you absorb
> and burn more carbohydrate per hour than glucose alone can manage.

## Why it matters

- It's the reason race fueling can reach **60–90 g/h** instead of stalling at ~60 g/h
  (see the targets in `[[carbohydrate-fueling]]` and `[[fueling-by-session-type]]`).
- Splitting the load across two transporters also tends to **reduce GI distress** at high intakes.

## Key facts

- **Glucose** is absorbed via the **SGLT1** transporter, which **saturates at ~60 g/h** — extra
  glucose then just sits in the gut and causes distress.
- Adding **fructose**, absorbed via a *different* transporter (**GLUT5**), opens a second pathway,
  raising usable exogenous-carb oxidation to **~90 g/h** (some trained athletes report ~105–120 g/h).
- Mechanism + ceilings are **established sports-nutrition consensus (Jeukendrup and colleagues)** —
  *not* stated on `[[src-maurten-fuel-guides]]`, which only gives the practical 60–90 g/h target.
- Maurten's formulation pairs the two sugars at roughly a **0.8 : 1 glucose : fructose** ratio
  (their product framing; verify against current product specs before quoting precisely).
