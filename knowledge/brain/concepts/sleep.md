# Sleep

> The single largest lever for **restitution** — where most adaptation, repair, and consolidation
> happen. Under-sleeping undercuts everything else in `[[recovery-and-adaptation]]`.

## Why it matters

- Sleep drives recovery and performance and lowers **injury and illness** risk.
- It is commonly the weak link: per `[[src-olt-sleep]]`, **>50% of Norwegian Olympic athletes**
  report periods of poor sleep.

## Key facts — per `[[src-olt-sleep]]`

- **Duration:** adults **7–9 h**; athletes likely need **toward 9–10 h**, especially in high-load
  periods. **Short naps** are fine if they don't harm night sleep.
- **Hygiene:** consistent **wake time**, daylight by day, a cool/dark/quiet room; limit alcohol,
  caffeine, large meals, screens, and emotional stimulation before bed.
- **Trackers:** don't lean on smartwatch sleep scores without professional oversight — accuracy is
  poor and they can breed anxiety that *worsens* sleep.
- **Reassurance:** one bad night before a big competition won't ruin performance; only **chronic**
  sleep problems need intervention.

> [!tip] Travel
> Crossing time zones wrecks sleep first — pair this with `[[time-zone-acclimatization]]` before
> competition abroad.
