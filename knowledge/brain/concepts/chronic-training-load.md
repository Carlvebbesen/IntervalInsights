# Chronic Training Load (CTL) — "Fitness"

> A slow **~42-day rolling average of daily `[[training-stress-score]]`** — the PMC's proxy for
> the durable fitness/`[[aerobic-base]]` you've banked.

## Why it matters

- Represents the cumulative effect of "consistency, duration and intensity of the last 6 weeks to
  several months or more." It rises and falls slowly, so it rewards **steady, repeatable training**
  over heroic single weeks — the quantitative face of `[[progressive-overload]]`.
- It is the fitness half of `[[training-stress-balance]]`: **"positive form is only useful if you
  have built the CTL to back it up."**

## Key facts

- **~6-week window** because "the body absorbs training adaptations with a roughly 6-week delay"
  (`[[src-trainerroad-tss-pmc]]`) — and, by the same logic, hard work done **inside** the last 6
  weeks before a race "may not manifest on race day."
- CTL **quantifies your training, not your body's response** to it — a high number is potential
  fitness, not guaranteed form.
- You **don't need huge weekly TSS** to build it: low-volume athletes can grow good fitness on
  ~**3 days/week**.
- **Typical reference values** (`[[src-icusync-ctl-atl-tsb]]`): recreational (4–5 h/wk) ≈ **40–60**;
  committed amateurs (8–12 h/wk) ≈ **70–100**; elites **>150**. Treat as orientation, not targets.
