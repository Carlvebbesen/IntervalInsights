# Iron Status

> Iron carries oxygen in the blood (haemoglobin) and muscle (myoglobin); endurance athletes —
> **women especially** — are prone to depletion that quietly erodes aerobic performance.

## Why it matters

- O₂ transport underpins `[[vo2max]]` and threshold work. Low iron → low haemoglobin → less O₂ to
  muscle → flat, heavy training *before* clinical anaemia ever shows.
- Endurance loses iron through **sweat, GI bleeding, foot-strike haemolysis**, and (women)
  menstruation — and athletes in low `[[energy-availability]]` take in less iron to begin with.

## Key facts — per `[[src-olt-iron]]`

- **Intake:** women **12–18 mg/day**; men **12 mg/day** (endurance men benefit from **~18 mg/day**).
- **Absorption:** **vitamin C** (citrus, berries, peppers, broccoli) boosts it; **tea/coffee inhibit**
  it — keep them **≥1 h** away from iron-rich meals.
- **Supplement only on a confirmed deficiency** (ferritin / haemoglobin test), under medical
  supervision; oral preferred over injection — see `[[dietary-supplements]]`.
- **Altitude raises iron demand** (red-cell synthesis) — be iron-replete *before* an
  `[[altitude-training]]` block (general sports-science; not stated in the source).

> [!warning] Don't blind-dose iron
> The body can't excrete excess iron — over-supplementing risks **iron poisoning** and gives **no
> benefit without deficiency**. Test first, then treat.
