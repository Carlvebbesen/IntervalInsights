# Bone Health

> Strong bone is built by **mechanical loading + adequate fuelling**; the dominant threat in
> athletes is **low `[[energy-availability]]`**, not lack of impact.

## Why it matters

- Stress fractures and early osteoporosis end seasons. The pathway runs through fuelling, not just
  training: chronic under-eating → menstrual dysfunction → low estrogen → bone loss.
- Distance runners combine high skeletal stress with a high risk of under-fuelling — the worst
  combination for bone.

## Key facts — per `[[src-olt-bone-health]]`

- **Calcium: 800–900 mg/day** (standard); **1,500 mg/day** for amenorrheic athletes.
- **Vitamin D** via fish oil / supplement (no specific dose given) — see `[[dietary-supplements]]`.
- **Loading builds bone:** weight-bearing and resistance training protect it; **heavy weights beat
  light** (links to `[[muscle-growth]]`).
- Low `[[energy-availability]]` drives bone loss **independent of age**; non-weight-bearing sites
  lose the most in amenorrheic athletes.

> [!warning] Red flags for the coach
> Amenorrhea **≥4–6 months** warrants medical follow-up. Bone lost after **~age 25** is hard to
> regain — protect energy availability *now*, not later. This is the bone arm of the Female Athlete
> Triad / RED-S (see `[[energy-availability]]`).
