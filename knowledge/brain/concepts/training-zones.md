# Training Zones

> A model that divides exercise intensity into discrete bands so training stress can be prescribed and tracked.

## Why it matters

- Zones turn "easy/hard" into something measurable and repeatable.
- They anchor the intensity distribution targets in `[[polarized-80-20]]`.

## The 5-zone model (anchored to thresholds)

| Zone | Name | Boundary | Feel (`[[rpe]]`) |
|------|------|----------|------------------|
| Z1 | Recovery | below aerobic threshold (LT1) | very easy, 1–2 |
| Z2 | Endurance | up to LT1 | easy, conversational, 3–4 |
| Z3 | Tempo | LT1 → LT2 ("the middle") | moderate-hard, 5–6 |
| Z4 | Threshold | around LT2 / `[[lactate-threshold]]` | hard, 7–8 |
| Z5 | VO₂max+ | above LT2, toward `[[vo2max]]` | very hard, 9–10 |

## Lactate-anchored zones (the threshold model)

The `[[kenyan-training]]` / `[[norwegian-method]]` school anchors zones to **measured lactate
values** rather than feel (`[[lactate-testing]]`). A long-distance runner's scheme:

| Zone | Lactate (mmol/L) | Use |
|------|------------------|-----|
| Z1 | <1.5 | easy |
| Z2 | 1.5–2.0 | steady |
| **Z3 (LT)** | **2.0–3.0** | the sub-threshold base — most quality work |
| Z4 | 3.0–8.0 | track sessions |
| Z5 | >8.0 | tempo / lactic, race season |

> [!note] The "controlled threshold sweet spot" (2–3 mmol/L) sits at the **Z3/low-Z4 boundary**
> of the threshold-anchored model above. This brain **trains there by default** — see
> `[[polarized-80-20]]` (house default: threshold-first), `[[norwegian-method]]`.

Frank Evertsen's Kenyan study uses a similar **S0–S4** scheme (warm-up <0.8 → very-high ≥5.0
mmol/L); see `[[src-evertsen-kenyan-physiology]]`.

## Key facts

- Zone boundaries are best set from measured thresholds/lactate, not %HRmax guesses. On power,
  the same boundary is `[[functional-threshold-power]]`, which scales **Intensity Factor** and
  `[[training-stress-score|TSS]]`.
- The three-zone view (below LT1 / LT1–LT2 / above LT2) underlies polarized models (`[[src-seiler-2010]]`).
