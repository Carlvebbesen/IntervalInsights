# Lactose Intolerance

> A reduced ability to digest milk sugar (lactose) — *not* a milk allergy. Most affected people
> tolerate **some** dairy, so blanket elimination is usually unnecessary and costs calcium.

## Why it matters

- Undigested lactose ferments in the large intestine → bloating, gas, **diarrhea**; for athletes,
  diarrhea also risks **dehydration** (`[[hydration]]`).
- Over-eliminating dairy strips quality protein, energy, and **calcium** from the diet — manage it
  within `[[meal-pattern]]`, don't just cut.

## Key facts — per `[[src-olt-lactose-intolerance]]`

- **Rare in ethnic Norwegians (~2–4%).** Tolerance is individual — typically **~1 dl milk or half
  a yogurt per meal** is fine.
- **Lower-lactose options:** fermented dairy (yogurt, soured milk) and **aged cheeses**; enzyme
  aids (Kerutab/Kerulac). **Avoid brunost and prim** (very high lactose).
- **Calcium:** still aim for **~3 dairy units/day** or fortified alternatives.
- **Secondary** lactose intolerance (after gut damage from `[[gluten-and-celiac]]` or infection) is
  often **reversible**; `[[probiotics]]` may help gut comfort.
