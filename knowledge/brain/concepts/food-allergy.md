# Food Allergy & Training

> A true immune reaction to a food. The athlete's task: **diagnose properly, then replace the lost
> nutrients** so the diet stays complete — elimination without replacement quietly hurts training.

## Why it matters

- Cutting a basis food removes the nutrients it carried; uncompensated, that erodes performance and
  health — fold replacements into `[[meal-pattern]]`.
- Self-diagnosis is rampant and usually wrong, leading to needless restriction.

## Key facts — per `[[src-olt-food-allergy]]`

- **Diagnose first:** ~**20%** of Norwegian adults *think* they react to food; only **2–5%** have a
  true allergy. Unvalidated tests (e.g. hair analysis) are "totally misleading" — confirm with
  qualified health personnel.
- **Common basis allergens → what they supply:** milk (energy, calcium, iodine, protein), egg,
  fish (omega-3, vitamin D), wheat/gluten (`[[gluten-and-celiac]]`), raw fruit/veg (pollen
  cross-reactivity — often fine **cooked**).
- **Replace it:** drop dairy → calcium + fortified products; fish-allergic → **1 tbsp cod-liver
  oil/day** (omega-3 + vitamin D) and fish ≥2×/week for iodine where possible.
- **Practical:** learn label terms (e.g. "myse" = whey); pre-arrange safe food when travelling
  (`[[travel-nutrition]]`).

> [!note] Allergy ≠ intolerance
> A food allergy is immune-mediated; `[[lactose-intolerance]]` is enzymatic. They're managed differently.
