# Protein for Athletes

> Protein repairs and remodels muscle and supports adaptation; needs are higher than for sedentary
> people but **easily met from ordinary food** — so supplements are rarely necessary.

## Why it matters

- Training raises muscle protein turnover; adequate protein preserves lean mass and enables the
  adaptation that the session triggers (pairs with `[[post-training-nutrition]]` and
  `[[muscle-growth]]`).
- Endurance athletes can *under*-eat protein if they crowd it out with carbohydrate — a real risk
  in low `[[energy-availability]]`.

## Key facts — per `[[src-olt-protein]]`

- **Daily target (g/kg body weight):** endurance **1.2–1.6**, strength/power **1.2–1.8**, young
  athletes in growth **2.0**.
- **Distribution beats a single hit:** aim for protein **in every meal, plus before and after
  training** (≈ a 10 g serving = 50 g fish or 220 g cooked pasta) — spread it, via `[[meal-pattern]]`.
- Most athletes already get **~15% of energy** from protein → needs covered; **amino-acid
  supplements have no documented effect** (see `[[dietary-supplements]]`).

> [!warning] More is not better
> Intakes **>3 g/kg/day** have no long-term safety data, can raise **urinary calcium loss**, and
> worsen existing kidney disease. Hit the target, then stop.
