# Performance Nutrition

> **Adapting nutrition to the physical work you do** — to optimise training, recovery, and
> competition *and* long-term health. The root concept of the brain's nutrition cluster.

## Why it matters

- Per `[[src-olt-performance-nutrition-pod]]` (Ina Garthe, Olympiatoppen): performance nutrition
  "springs from sports physiology — you adapt nutrition to the physical work you will do" — but it
  is "much more than that," spanning health, immunity, and body composition.
- It is the lever that lets a session's intended stimulus actually be hit and absorbed: the
  `[[fuel-for-the-work-required]]` guardrail, the carbs in `[[carbohydrate-fueling]]`, the
  `[[protein-for-athletes]]` for repair, and `[[hydration]]` all ladder up to here.

## How the brain organises it

- **Daily base:** `[[meal-pattern]]` (regular meals, food variety) on top of adequate
  `[[energy-availability]]` (eat enough for the load).
- **Around sessions (timing):** `[[pre-training-nutrition]]` → `[[during-training-nutrition]]` →
  `[[post-training-nutrition]]`; per-session detail lives in `[[fueling-by-session-type]]`.
- **For competition:** `[[glycogen-loading]]` and `[[competition-day-nutrition]]`.
- **Health & specifics:** `[[iron-status]]`, `[[bone-health]]`, `[[dietary-supplements]]`, and the
  caution against restrictive `[[alternative-diets]]`.
