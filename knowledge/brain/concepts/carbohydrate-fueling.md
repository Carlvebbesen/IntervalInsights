# Carbohydrate Fueling

> Carbohydrate is the dominant fuel for moderate-to-high intensity exercise; taking it in
> *during* long or hard sessions sustains pace and protects training quality.

## Why it matters

- Muscle + liver **glycogen is limited** — roughly **~90 min** of moderate-to-high intensity work
  before depletion starts to degrade output. Carbohydrate intake spares glycogen and delays "the wall."
- Underfueling a key session means a **junk session**: pace fades, intensity can't be held, and the
  intended `[[lactate-threshold]]` / `[[vo2max]]` stimulus is blunted (and recovery is worse).
- The longer and harder the session, the bigger the payoff — which is the basis of the
  `[[fuel-for-the-work-required]]` guardrail and the per-session map in `[[fueling-by-session-type]]`.

## Key facts — how much (per `[[src-maurten-fuel-guides]]`)

| Session | Carb target |
|---|---|
| Short / easy, **< ~60–75 min** | little to none needed |
| Endurance **~1–2.5 h** | **30–60 g/h** |
| Long efforts **> 2.5 h** / racing | up to **60–90 g/h** |

- The **~60 g/h ceiling** for a single sugar comes from gut-transport limits; pushing to ~90 g/h
  needs **glucose + fructose together** → see `[[multiple-transportable-carbohydrates]]`.
- **The gut is trainable.** Tolerance to high carb intake improves with practice ("train the gut"),
  so race-day intakes should be rehearsed in training (sports-nutrition consensus; not in the source).
- **Format** (drink / gel / solid) is mostly preference + practicality; a carb drink also supplies
  fluid — pair fueling with `[[hydration]]`.
- Carbohydrate is **not** the only consideration for very hard short efforts — buffering acidosis
  with `[[bicarbonate]]` is the relevant pre-session aid there, not in-session carbs.
- **Daily intake (not just per-hour):** total carbohydrate is periodised to the load — roughly
  **5–7 g/kg/day** (lighter days) up to **10–12 g/kg/day** (extreme load), per
  `[[src-olt-glycogen-loading]]`; the pre-race build-up is `[[glycogen-loading]]`.
- **Timing matters too:** `[[pre-training-nutrition]]` → `[[during-training-nutrition]]` →
  `[[post-training-nutrition]]` for *when*, and `[[meal-pattern]]` for the daily base.
