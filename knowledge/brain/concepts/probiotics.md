# Probiotics

> Live bacteria that, in adequate dose, support digestive comfort and **intestinal immune**
> function — most useful around **travel** and **antibiotics**, not as a routine for healthy athletes.

## Why it matters

- A settled gut protects training and competition — the main athlete use case is **preventing
  travel gut trouble** (`[[travel-nutrition]]`) and recovering flora after antibiotics.

## Key facts — per `[[src-olt-probiotics]]`

- **Effective dose: >1 billion (10⁹) CFU/day** ≈ **1 glass Biola or Cultura**.
- **Travel protocol:** to high-risk areas, **≥100 ml Biola/day** starting **~1 week before** and
  through the stay.
- **Strains vary by product** (e.g. Biola: *L. rhamnosus GG*, *L. acidophilus LA-5*, *B. lactis
  Bb12*).
- Evidence: normalises flora after antibiotics, shortens/reduces antibiotic-associated diarrhea,
  stimulates gut immunity. May ease comfort in `[[lactose-intolerance]]`.
- **Not permanent:** bacteria don't colonise — the effect fades **1–2 weeks** after stopping, so
  benefit needs continued daily intake.

> [!note] Who should skip it
> Healthy athletes with a good diet may not need supplementation; avoid in the critically
> ill/immunocompromised. See `[[dietary-supplements]]` for the food-first stance.
