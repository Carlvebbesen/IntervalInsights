# Antioxidants

> Compounds that neutralise exercise-induced free radicals. Get them from **food**: high-dose
> antioxidant *supplements* show no performance benefit and can **blunt training adaptation**.

## Why it matters

- Training raises free-radical production — but that oxidative signal is **part of how the body
  adapts**. Mopping it up with mega-dose pills can work against the very adaptation you trained for
  (`[[recovery-and-adaptation]]`).

## Key facts — per `[[src-olt-antioxidants]]`

- **Food target:** **≥5 portions/day (~750 g)** — 2 fruit + 3 vegetables, incl. 1 citrus
  (portion = 1 fruit / 1 glass juice / 1–2 dl berries / 150 g cooked veg / 1 salad bowl).
- Research has **not** shown a benefit from antioxidant supplements, and **some studies suggest
  they are counterproductive**. Whole foods carry thousands of phytochemicals acting
  synergistically — "supplements cannot replace fruit and vegetables."
- High-dose isolated vitamins/minerals **interact and reduce intestinal absorption** of other
  nutrients (see `[[dietary-supplements]]`).

> [!warning] Don't mega-dose vitamin C/E around training
> Especially in hard adaptation blocks — high-dose antioxidant supplements can dampen the
> mitochondrial/aerobic adaptation signal (sports-science consensus; OLT: they "may work against
> the purpose"). Eat the produce instead, via `[[meal-pattern]]`.
