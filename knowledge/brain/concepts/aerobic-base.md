# Aerobic Base

> The capacity for sustained low-intensity work, built by high volumes of easy (Z1–Z2) training.

## Why it matters

- The foundation everything else is built on: mitochondrial density, capillarization, fat
  oxidation, and the ability to absorb hard training without breaking down.
- A bigger base raises both `[[lactate-threshold]]` and the durability of `[[vo2max]]` efforts.

## Key facts

- Built primarily in Z1–Z2 (`[[training-zones]]`), which is why volume here dominates in
  `[[polarized-80-20]]` and `[[polarized-training]]`.
- Adaptations are slow and cumulative — base is a multi-month/year project, not a block. This is
  what a slow-moving `[[chronic-training-load]]` (CTL, ~42-day average) tries to proxy.
- High volume is necessary but **not sufficient**: the Norwegian/Bakken view is that better
  athletes gain less from pure easy volume and must add controlled threshold work.

## "Base" ≠ slow jogging

> [!note] Many greats built their base on **steady sub-threshold running**, not only slow
> jogging. `[[grete-waitz]]` and Ingrid Kristiansen *"almost never saw the 4-something/km"* on
> "easy" days (AT ~3:25/km); Kenyans' long run ("footing") finishes *at* LT. This is the
> upper-aerobic end of the base — still below the threshold sessions, but far from a jog. Keep
> genuinely easy days easy *as well* (`[[recovery-and-adaptation]]`); the two coexist.
