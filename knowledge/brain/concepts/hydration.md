# Hydration

> Maintaining fluid (and electrolyte) balance before, during, and after exercise. Dehydration drops
> plasma volume → less muscle blood flow, more heat strain → measurably worse endurance, strength,
> power, and cognition.

## Why it matters

- Fluid loss lowers plasma volume, which **cuts stroke volume and raises heart rate and core
  temperature** — performance falls progressively as the deficit grows (`[[src-olt-fluid-balance]]`).
- Fluid and fuel travel together: a carbohydrate-electrolyte drink delivers both, so hydration and
  `[[carbohydrate-fueling]]` are planned as one across the `[[during-training-nutrition]]` /
  `[[post-training-nutrition]]` timeline.

## Key facts — per `[[src-olt-fluid-balance]]`

- **Daily baseline (non-training): 2–3 L/day.**
- **During exercise:** start within **15 min**; **~150–300 ml every 15–20 min** (≈ **0.5–1 L/h**),
  adjusted for heat and intensity. Prefer **cold drinks (<15 °C)**, especially in heat.
- **For efforts > 1 h:** use a **sports drink (carbs + electrolytes)**, not plain water — it
  improves gut absorption and fluid retention.
- **After:** replace **~150% of the fluid lost within 2 h**; **sodium-containing** drinks rehydrate
  better than water (low-sodium fluid just makes you pee it out) — see `[[post-training-nutrition]]`.
- **Body-mass loss thresholds:** keep losses under **~3–4%** (temperate) / **~2%** (hot); **>4%**
  raises risk of vomiting, diarrhea, and heat illness. Performance already degrades around **~2–3%**.
- **Measure it:** weigh before/after (dry, minimal clothing). Fluid loss = weight change + fluid
  drunk during the session.

## Warnings

> [!warning] Both directions are dangerous
> **Thirst lags** real losses — don't wait for it. But **don't over-drink plain water** either:
> diluting blood sodium risks **hyponatremia**. And you **cannot train** the body to tolerate
> dehydration.

- **"Salty sweaters"** (high sweat-sodium) need extra sodium **before, during, and after** — a
  candidate for a future sweat-test deepening of this page.
