# Descending km Intervals with Float

> A long descending ladder of km reps separated by a 1 km **float** (controlled, faster-than-jog) recovery — high time at marathon/threshold intensity.

> [!warning] Demanding session — high volume at intensity. Assumes a well-developed `[[aerobic-base]]` and consistent, high-mileage training. Not for beginners.

```interval
discipline: run
reps: variable (descending km ladder)
work: 7-6-5-4-3-2 km  (variant: 6-5-4-3-2-1 km)
work_intensity: marathon → threshold pace
recovery: 1 km float
recovery_intensity: Z2 (controlled, faster than jog)
sets: 1
target: marathon-pace endurance / lactate-threshold
notes: float pauses keep cumulative intensity high; alternate float / normal pause weeks
```

## Variants

- `7-6-5-4-3-2 km` with 1 km float between reps.
- `6-5-4-3-2-1 km` with 1 km float between reps.

## Purpose

- Accumulates large volume near marathon/`[[lactate-threshold]]` pace. The float recovery
  (see `[[pace-terminology]]`) keeps the aerobic system loaded throughout — a big
  marathon-specific stimulus. Fits the hard 20% of `[[polarized-training]]`.

## Execution notes

- Hold the work reps at marathon→threshold pace (`[[training-zones]]` Z3–Z4); the float is
  controlled, not a recovery jog.
- Note: alternate weeks of float vs. normal (easy) pauses.
