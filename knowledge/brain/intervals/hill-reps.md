# Hill Reps ("X element")

> Short, steep hill repetitions — the weekly **"X element"** of the [[norwegian-method]]: a
> stimulus just above threshold with **big fiber recruitment but little flat-running wear**.

```interval
discipline: run
reps: 10-14 (short) ; or a set of long hills
work: 120-200 m (short, steep) or 600-800 m (long)
work_intensity: above threshold, ~5-8 mmol/L
recovery: jog/walk down
recovery_intensity: easy
sets: 1
target: neuromuscular + economy + vo2max
notes: Peter Coe used 180-200 m steep + 600-800 m long; the Ingebrigtsens run Saturday hills; Kenyan youth do 120-200 m x14; Moroccan "power" = 10x300 m hill
```

## Purpose

- A controlled dose of high-end work that develops `[[running-economy]]` and recruits muscle
  fibers *without* the wear-and-tear of flat high-speed sessions — the once-weekly change of
  stimulus inside an otherwise threshold-dominant block.

## Execution notes

- Run the hill **strong but controlled**, easy jog-down recovery. Placed on a non-double-threshold
  day (often Saturday). One of several "X element" options (the other being short fast track reps
  at 5–8 mmol/L).
