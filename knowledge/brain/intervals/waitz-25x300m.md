# Waitz 25 × 300 m

> Twenty-five 300 m reps off a tiny **15 s recovery** — a high-density short-interval session
> from [[grete-waitz]]'s 1970s track training.

> [!warning] Demanding session — high rep volume at intensity. Assumes a well-developed `[[aerobic-base]]` and consistent, high-mileage training.

```interval
discipline: run
reps: 25
work: 300 m
work_intensity: fast but controlled (the 15 s rest keeps it aerobic, not all-out)
recovery: 15 s
recovery_intensity: easy
sets: 1
target: vo2max / economy / speed-endurance
notes: from Grete Waitz's Feb 1974 diary; she paired it with 4x1000 m (1 min rest) and 1000-600-300 ladders
```

## Purpose

- Lots of fast, short turnover at a tiny recovery builds `[[vo2max]]`, economy and
  speed-endurance while the short rest keeps the aerobic demand high across all 25 reps.

## Execution notes

- Even, repeatable pace — the point is density, not max speed per rep. A historical cousin of
  the modern double-threshold evening session (short reps, very short rest). Cf. `[[400m-reps]]`.
