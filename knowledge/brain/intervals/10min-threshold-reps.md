# 10-min Threshold Reps (4–6 × 10 min)

> Long, controlled sub-threshold reps — the **morning staple of a [[double-threshold]] day** and
> the canonical Kenyan / NTNU threshold session.

```interval
discipline: run
reps: 4-6
work: 10 min
work_intensity: sub-threshold, 2-3 mmol/L (~80-87% HRmax)
recovery: 1 min
recovery_intensity: easy jog
sets: 1
target: lactate-threshold
notes: morning double-threshold session; NTNU 2024 used 6x10 min (and split 3+3x10); Kenyan 4-5x10
```

## Purpose

- Maximize time at **controlled threshold** to raise `[[lactate-threshold]]` and
  `[[fractional-utilization]]` at low muscular cost. The gentlest, most repeatable threshold
  session in the [[norwegian-method]].

## Execution notes

- Keep lactate **≤ 3 mmol/L by the last rep** — drifting above means it was too fast (back off).
- Even pacing; long reps deliberately keep `[[muscle-tone]]` moderated so a second (evening)
  session is still possible. Pair with shorter, faster reps in the evening (e.g. `[[1000m-reps]]`).
- **Fuel quality work:** carbs beforehand, and 1–2 sips of drink mix between reps help hold the
  band on a glycogen-depleted morning (`[[fueling-by-session-type]]`).
