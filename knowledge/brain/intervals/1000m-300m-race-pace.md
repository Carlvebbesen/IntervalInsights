# Race-Pace Test — 1000s + 300s

> 4–6 × 1000 m then 3–7 × 300 m at race pace, **controlled by the lactate curve** — Bob
> Kennedy's session, adapted by [[marius-bakken]] as a fitness check ~4 days before a race.

> [!warning] Demanding session — race-pace intensity. Assumes a well-developed `[[aerobic-base]]` and sharpening phase. Not a base-period workout.

```interval
discipline: run
reps: 4-6 x 1000 m, then 3-7 x 300 m
work: 1000 m, then 300 m
work_intensity: ~3k-5k race pace (lactate-controlled, not pace-forced)
recovery: short (controlled)
sets: 1
target: race-specific sharpening / vo2max + economy
notes: Kennedy did 6x1000 + 300 @ ~3k pace; Bakken's Rome 5k PB build hit 1000s in 2:32.8 / 2:32.3 / 2:32.3 with a near-flat lactate curve between reps 2-3
```

## Purpose

- Sharpen **race-specific** recruitment *and read your fitness*: a **flat lactate curve** across
  the 1000s (rising pace, stable lactate) means you are ready — *"one of the most potent ways
  of actually knowing where you are."*

## Execution notes

- Control by the lactate curve, not by forcing the pace. Recovery long enough to hold quality.
- Used sparingly in the race phase, not in the threshold base. Contrast the steady-rise
  `[[descending-1600-400-track]]`.
