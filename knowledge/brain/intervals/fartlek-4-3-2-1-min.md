# Fartlek 4 × (4-3-2-1 min)

> A fartlek of four descending 4-3-2-1 min surges with a flat 60 s recovery.

> [!warning] Demanding session — high volume at intensity. Assumes a well-developed `[[aerobic-base]]` and consistent, high-mileage training. Not for beginners.

```interval
discipline: run
reps: 4 sets of (4, 3, 2, 1 min)
work: 4 / 3 / 2 / 1 min
work_intensity: hard, toward 10k / VO2max pace
recovery: 60 s (P60)
recovery_intensity: easy jog
sets: 4
target: vo2max
notes: "P60" = 60 s pause
```

## Purpose

- High-intensity aerobic development targeting `[[vo2max]]`. The short, constant 60 s
  recovery keeps cumulative load high. Pace refs in `[[pace-terminology]]`.

## Kenyan / Moroccan race-pace variant

- The same descending-fartlek idea appears as the Moroccan "race-pace" session
  **6-5-4-3-2-1 min** (coach Abdelkader Kada, El Guerrouj's group;
  `[[src-bakken-kenyan-training-guide]]`). Its track-based cousin is the
  `[[descending-1600-400-track]]` ladder — both aim for a **steady, controlled rise** in effort
  across the set rather than an early spike (see `[[kenyan-training]]`).
