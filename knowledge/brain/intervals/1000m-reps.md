# 6-12 × 1000 m

> Six to twelve 1 km reps with a 60–90 s recovery — a classic threshold/VO₂max workhorse.

> [!warning] Demanding session — high volume at intensity. Assumes a well-developed `[[aerobic-base]]` and consistent, high-mileage training. Not for beginners.

```interval
discipline: run
reps: 6-12
work: 1000 m
work_intensity: threshold → 10k pace
recovery: 60 s or 90 s (P60 / P90)
recovery_intensity: easy jog
sets: 1
target: lactate-threshold / vo2max
notes: shorter rest (60 s) raises the aerobic demand
```

## Purpose

- Scalable km reps to build `[[lactate-threshold]]` and `[[vo2max]]`; rep count and rest set
  the difficulty. Pace refs in `[[pace-terminology]]`.

## The double-threshold evening session

- Run **controlled — 10–12 × 1000 m at ~3.0 mmol/L** — this is the classic **Thursday-evening**
  short-threshold session of a `[[double-threshold]]` day (paired with `[[10min-threshold-reps]]`
  in the morning) in the `[[norwegian-method]]` (`[[src-bakken-norwegian-model-revisited]]`).
  Faster, shorter reps than the morning, still capped at threshold — not a VO₂max session.
