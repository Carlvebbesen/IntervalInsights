## Use of Artificial Intelligence (AI)

The Service uses a third-party AI model — **OpenAI's GPT-4o-mini**, accessed via OpenAI's API — to analyze Your training data. The AI classifies the type of workout, identifies interval structures, and generates readable summaries. AI analysis is a core feature of the Service.

Because AI analysis is essential to delivering the Service, the legal basis is performance of a contract (Art 6(1)(b)) rather than consent that can be freely withdrawn. To stop AI processing of Your data, You must delete Your account or disconnect Strava.

We apply the following technical safeguards when sending data to the AI provider:

- **No directly identifying information is sent to the AI provider.** We do not send Your name, email address, IP address, Clerk user ID, internal user UUID, or Strava athlete ID.
- The data sent is limited to numerical and structural training data — for example, time-series of velocity and distance, and lap structures — together with the activity sport type. This data is not, on its own, sufficient to identify You.
- **Heart-rate data is sent to the AI provider only if You have enabled the *Heart-rate processing* toggle** in Your account settings (default: off). When the toggle is off, heart-rate is not fetched from Strava, not stored, and not included in AI prompts.
- AI inference runs on infrastructure operated by OpenAI, which may be located in the **United States**.
- We use the standard OpenAI API. Under OpenAI's API data policy, data submitted via the API is **not used to train or improve OpenAI models** by default, and is retained by OpenAI only for limited abuse-monitoring purposes.

The AI does not make legal or similarly significant decisions about You within the meaning of GDPR Art 22. The classification of training types is a form of profiling, but it is informational and does not produce legal effects.

## Optional Integrations

The Service offers optional integrations with third-party platforms that provide additional analytics features. These integrations are **off by default** and only become active if You explicitly opt in.

### intervals.icu

If You opt in to the *intervals.icu* integration, the Service will exchange training data with [intervals.icu](https://intervals.icu) to provide enhanced training analytics.

- **Prerequisites.** This integration requires (a) the *Heart-rate processing* toggle to be ON, and (b) You to have Your own account at intervals.icu and to provide credentials authorizing the Service to communicate with that account.
- **Separate controller.** intervals.icu is operated by a third party and acts as an independent Data Controller for the data You hold in their service. By connecting Your intervals.icu account You also accept intervals.icu's own privacy policy and terms — see https://intervals.icu/privacy-policy.
- **Data exchanged.** When this integration is active, the Service may send Your training and heart-rate data to intervals.icu and may retrieve analytics derived from that data.
- **Withdrawal.** You may disconnect the intervals.icu integration at any time from Your account settings. Disconnecting stops further data exchange but does not, on its own, delete data already stored in Your intervals.icu account — for that, contact intervals.icu directly.

If You do not opt in to this integration, no data is sent to intervals.icu.

## Deletion of Your Personal Data

You can stop processing of Your Personal Data through any of the following actions:

1. **Turn off the *Heart-rate processing* toggle** in Your account settings to stop further heart-rate processing while keeping the rest of Your account. To also delete heart-rate data already stored, delete Your account (option 2 below).
2. **Delete Your account** from the *Settings* page in the app. This deletes Your user record and all associated activities and interval segments from our database, and revokes Your Strava authorization.
3. **Disconnect Your Strava account** from within Strava's settings (Strava → Settings → My Apps → Revoke Access). Strava notifies the Service via a deauthorization webhook, after which Your Strava OAuth tokens are removed and the Service can no longer access Your Strava data.
4. **Contact Us by email** to request deletion. We will action the request within one month, as required by GDPR Art 12(3).

After deletion, Personal Data may persist for a limited period in encrypted backups and in any records We are legally required to retain.
